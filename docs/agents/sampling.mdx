Leash sets sampling **per turn**, keyed on whether `<think>` reasoning is on. This follows Qwen3's published best practice — and pointedly avoids the serve's near-greedy default, which Qwen explicitly warns against.

## Per thinking mode

Every text and tool route gets sampling chosen by the turn's `thinking` flag:

| Mode | Temperature | top-p | When |
|------|-------------|-------|------|
| **Thinking** (`<think>` on) | **0.6** | **0.95** | Deep-text turns |
| **Non-thinking** (`/no_think`) | **0.7** | **0.8** | Standard turns |

This is the Qwen3 recommendation for each mode. `vision` (qwen3vl, single-shot) keeps its own behavior and is left out of this mapping; every text/tool route gets proper sampling.

## Why not the serve default

The `qvac serve` default is **near-greedy (temp ~0.1)** — and that is exactly what Qwen3 warns against.

<Warning>
Greedy decoding on Qwen3 causes **performance degradation and endless repetitions / loops**. Leash never uses it; the per-mode sampling above is applied on every non-vision turn for this reason.
</Warning>

## Why top-k is omitted

There is no `topK` in the sampling parameters. The `qvac`/`qwen3-4b` provider doesn't support it — it dropped the value and logged an AI SDK warning every turn — so it is intentionally left out rather than passed and discarded.

<Tip>
The two-mode split lines up with the runtime: the `thinking` call option that picks the temperature is the same flag that controls whether the model's `<think>…</think>` is extracted into the UI's Reasoning panel.
</Tip>
