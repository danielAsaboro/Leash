The short version: **Leash** is the assistant you touch; **Mycelium** is the mesh underneath; and the whole thing runs on your own hardware, offline, because that was the point.

## What is Leash?

**Leash** is the user-facing exocortex built on the **Mycelium** mesh. It's the AI that knows *you* — it remembers your notes, watches your screen activity, runs tools on your behalf, and checks in proactively when something needs your attention.

The name comes from a simple inversion: your AI should be on *your* leash, not the other way around. It does what you tell it, when you tell it, with your data, on your hardware. You hold the leash.

## What is Mycelium?

**Mycelium** is the mesh operating system underneath. Like the fungal network it's named after — the underground threads that connect the trees of a forest — Mycelium connects your devices into a private mesh that shares inference, memory, and compute. The mesh is invisible; Leash is the surface you touch.

## QVAC Hackathon I — "Unleash Edge AI"

Leash was built for **QVAC Hackathon I** (Tether), in the **June 1–21, 2026** build window. The theme — "Unleash Edge AI" — is exactly what it sounds like: build something real that runs entirely on-device, with no cloud AI APIs in the loop.

<Note>
**The three hard rules.** All inference goes through `@qvac/sdk` (no OpenAI, no Anthropic, no cloud calls). It works offline after the first-run model download. And it's licensed permissively — Leash standardizes on **Apache-2.0**.
</Note>

## The spike — proving the hard parts first

Before building the full system, the team ran a **spike**: de-risk the four hardest technical primitives before committing a week to each. All four passed on **2026-05-31**, unblocking the Week-1 build.

<Steps>
  <Step title="On-device inference + embeddings">
    `qwen3-4b` running locally through `@qvac/sdk`, with `gte-large` embeddings.
  </Step>
  <Step title="On-device RAG">
    A HyperDB graph store, indexed via those embeddings — retrieval with no server.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Encrypted P2P delegated compute">
    `hypha` routing completions across devices over an encrypted mesh.
  </Step>
  <Step title="On-device LoRA">
    QVAC Fabric fine-tuning a personalized adapter — improvement that never leaves the device.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Zero mocks: real inference, real RAG, real encrypted P2P, real LoRA. The spike set the bar everything since has been held to. (Full evidence lives in `submission/SPIKE_RESULTS.md`.)

## The five-layer architecture

Mycelium is organized into five layers, each a clean seam in the codebase:

| Layer | Package | Role |
|-------|---------|------|
| **Mesh** | `hypha` daemon | P2P connectivity, inference routing, the economy |
| **Senses** | screen watcher, audio, photos | Screen-activity capture, audio input, photo tagging |
| **Mind** | the agent | The tool loop, RAG council, routing |
| **Memory** | graph + notes | Graph store, notes index, consolidation |
| **Clients** | web / desktop / mobile | The surfaces users interact with |

The web client lives at `http://localhost:6801`.

## The constitution

The **constitution** is how Leash knows who you are — three markdown files you edit in **Brain → Constitution**:

- `soul.md` — who you are (background, values, context)
- `goals.md` — where you're going (≤5 current goals)
- `heartbeat.md` — what to watch for proactively

The agent folds *soul* and *goals* into every turn's system prompt, and the *heartbeat* checklist drives the autonomous proactive loop. See [Heartbeat](/agents/heartbeat).

## Why offline-first?

The standard AI-assistant model ships your conversations to a cloud backend. Leash doesn't. Your notes, chats, screen activity, and constitution files never leave your device — the AI runs on your GPU, knows your stuff, and nobody else can read it.

After a one-time warm-cache (downloading model weights), Leash works on an airplane with Wi-Fi off. "Works in airplane mode" is treated as an acceptance criterion, not a nice-to-have.
