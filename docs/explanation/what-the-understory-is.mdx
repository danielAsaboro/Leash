---
title: "What The Understory is"
description: "Why Mycelium includes an autonomous paper and what that surface proves about the platform."
---

The Understory is Mycelium's autonomous paper. It is not a decorative side feature. It
is one of the clearest demonstrations that the platform can do more than answer direct
chat questions.

## What it is doing

The newsroom runtime discovers leads, gathers research, drafts articles, reviews claims,
publishes editions, and can attach generated media such as hero art or audio when the
pipeline produces them.

## Why it matters

The Understory proves several parts of the system working together:

- background daemons stay alive and coordinated
- retrieval and source gathering are not limited to chat
- the model stack can produce structured publishable artifacts
- dossiers and source trails make the output inspectable

## Why it belongs inside Mycelium

An exocortex should do more than wait for prompts. The paper is one answer to that: a
surface where the system periodically synthesizes what matters in your world and presents
it back as an edition you can inspect.

## What makes it different from a generic news page

The Understory is grounded in the same local-first philosophy as the rest of Mycelium.
Its interesting property is not "there is a feed." It is that the feed is generated by
the same on-device, inspectable runtime that powers the rest of the platform.
