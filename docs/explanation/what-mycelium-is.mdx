title: "What Leash is"
description: "The product and the engine: one private intelligence spread across your own devices."
---

**Leash** is a private, end-to-end-encrypted assistant that runs on your own hardware. Its
job is not just to host a chatbot. It combines local inference, retrieval, typed memory,
delegated compute, and a nightly learning loop into one assistant that stays on your
devices.

**Mycelium** is the engine underneath. It is the local-first runtime, the encrypted device
mesh, the agent economy, and the workspace that builds them. Leash is the surface you use;
Mycelium is the five-layer system that makes Leash work and that other clients (the desktop
app, the hub and edge-node apps) also sit on.

## Product versus engine

It helps to keep the two words straight:

- **Leash** is the flagship interface — the assistant, dashboard, and operator console in
  `apps/web`, plus the desktop client in `apps/desktop`. When you "use the product," you use
  Leash.
- **Mycelium** is the platform boundary — the serve and model-alias layer, Hypha for pairing
  and delegated compute, the agent economy, and the `packages/*` that implement senses, mind,
  mesh, memory, and shared logic.

Treating Leash as the whole system would hide that boundary. The architecture, the mesh, and
the memory loop are Mycelium concerns; the chat, the catalog, and the settings tabs are Leash
surfaces over them.

## What makes it distinct

Three constraints shape the product:

- inference is QVAC-only
- the system is expected to work offline after warm-cache
- the runtime is allowed to use multiple of your own devices, but never third-party cloud AI

That combination changes the architecture. Models live in a local serve. Mesh routing is
between your machines. Retrieval is against local files and stores. When the system needs
another GPU — or a modality this device can't serve — it borrows one from a paired peer, not
from a hosted API.

## What the user is actually getting

From your point of view, Leash offers:

- grounded chat over private notes, activity, and past conversations
- delegated compute and modality borrowing over a paired device mesh
- an opt-in agent economy that settles borrowed compute on-chain
- typed memory plus retrieval-backed recall, and a nightly loop that adapts a personal model
- local speech, vision, and image paths
- a reproducible workspace where the core runtime is inspectable
