The Understory is a daily newspaper written about **your** world: an autonomous newsroom
daemon discovers leads from your context, researches, drafts, reviews, and publishes —
all with local models. Mission Control is where you watch (and unstick) the pipeline.

## Read today's edition

The dashboard's front page **is** the paper: lead story, sections (AI / Compute /
Solana / private briefs), date pagination, ⌘K full-text search. Every article carries
its **sources** sidebar, **unverified claims** flagged by the reviewer, a link to the
research **dossier** behind it, and a read-aloud button.

## Run the newsroom

The daemon ticks on a cadence (default 60 min): discovery pulls new leads, then each
story moves `QUEUED → RESEARCHING → RESEARCH_READY → DRAFTING → REVIEW → PUBLISHED`.
Start it alongside the stack and leave it running; the masthead dot shows its state.

## Watch the floor — /mission-control

- **Count cards:** queued / in progress / needs reporting / published.
- **Active assignment:** the story being worked right now, with its stage tracker.
- **Daemon telemetry:** next-discovery countdown, cadence, recent runs with ✓/✗.
- **In Production:** every mid-pipeline story, stalest first — each with its stage,
  how long it's been untouched, and **why it's stuck** (the latest failed run's reason,
  verbatim).

## Re-queue a stuck story

A story untouched for more than five minutes unlocks its **Re-queue** button: confirm,
and it returns to `QUEUED` for the daemon's next tick.

<Warning>
Re-queue restarts the **full pipeline** for that story — research, draft, and review are
redone, not resumed. Fresh rows keep the button disabled so you can't double-process a
story the daemon is actively working.
</Warning>
