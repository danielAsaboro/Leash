Use this guide when you want to inspect the publishing surface rather than the assistant
chat surface.

## Open the feed

Open `/feed`. This is The Understory, the paper produced by the newsroom runtime. The
latest edition opens first when one exists.

## Read an edition

Each edition page gives you:

- the masthead and newsroom status dot
- section filters such as AI, Compute, Solana, and Brief
- date navigation to older or newer editions
- the count of published and in-progress articles for that date

Use the arrows to move between editions, or navigate directly to `/feed/YYYY-MM-DD`.

## Open an article

Click a headline to open the full article. Depending on what the newsroom produced, an
article can include:

- headline and dek
- rendered markdown body
- generated hero image
- source list
- reviewer-raised unverified claims
- dossier link
- generated audio playback

This makes the feed one of the clearest showcase surfaces in the project: it ties
local models, research, review, memory, and publishing together visibly.

## Open the dossier when you want the trail

Use **Open dossier** on an article when you want the research path rather than the final
story. The dossier is the evidence surface: fetched sources, notes, and the claims the
pipeline tried to confirm.

## Understand the empty state

If there is no edition yet, the feed does not pretend. It shows that the press is
warming up.

That usually means one of two things:

- the newsroom is still producing stories
- the newsroom daemon is not running

When the daemon is down, the feed links you toward the controls that start it.
