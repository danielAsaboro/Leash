Speech never leaves your machine: `parakeet` transcribes locally and `supertonic`
speaks locally.

## Dictate into the chat

Click the microphone in the composer, speak, and stop — the transcript lands in the
input box for you to edit or send. The first use asks for browser microphone permission.

## Hold a voice call

Open the call overlay from the chat. Speak naturally; Leash answers aloud in short,
markdown-free sentences (spoken answers are deliberately brief — ask in text when you
want depth).

**Barge-in:** start talking while it's speaking — playback stops and it listens. The
interrupted generation completes silently in the background, so a follow-up may queue
for a few seconds; that trade is deliberate (see
[Architecture](/explanation/architecture)).

## Read an answer aloud

Every assistant message has a play button. Long answers are synthesized in chunks, so
playback starts before synthesis finishes.

## Approvals and forms during voice

Approval cards and in-chat forms are **not** spoken — answer them on screen. A voice
turn that triggers one will pause until you do.
