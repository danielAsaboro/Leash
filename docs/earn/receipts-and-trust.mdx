---
title: "Receipts and trust"
description: "How Leash records economy evidence for free and paid delegated inference."
---

Receipts make delegated compute auditable. They give the consumer, provider, and operator a shared record of what was selected, what was authorized, what was streamed, and what was settled.

## Receipt fields

A complete run can record:

- Mesh class: `private` or `public`.
- Provider identity and advertised name.
- Model alias and modality.
- Rate in micro-units per thousand tokens.
- Token cap and metered usage.
- Authorization id for paid routes.
- Settlement transaction for paid routes.
- Zero-cost close marker for free routes.
- Timing data such as TTFT and total duration.

Private mesh receipts use the same shape with a forced zero rate. That keeps history, analytics, and UI rendering consistent without charging personal devices.

## Trust inputs

Public routing uses more than price. A cheap provider can still lose if it is slow, unreliable, or has poor history.

| Signal | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Identity | Prevents anonymous capacity from blending into trusted private peers. |
| Reputation | Lets successful sessions improve future ranking. |
| Receipts | Proves what was charged or why a run was free. |
| Settlement result | Confirms paid runs closed on the expected rail. |
| Self-dealing checks | Detects when the same operator controls both sides of a paid route. |

## Paid evidence

Paid runs should leave two layers of evidence:

- **Runtime evidence** in the Leash and Hypha logs: quote, authorization, stream, meter, close.
- **Settlement evidence** on the configured payment rail: the final transaction or facilitator result.

The provider earns only after the metered close succeeds.

## Free evidence

Free runs still matter. They prove routing selected a public provider but did not charge:

- Public provider advertised a `0` rate.
- Consumer skipped payment authorization.
- Completion streamed normally.
- Receipt closed at `0`.

This is important for public providers that want to donate spare compute or build reputation before charging.
