---
title: "Receipts and trust"
description: "How Leash records paid settlement evidence today and how to validate zero-rate routes."
---

Receipts make paid delegated compute auditable. They give the consumer, provider, and operator a
shared record of what was selected, what was authorized, what was streamed, and what was settled.

## Receipt fields

A paid session receipt records:

- Mesh id and model alias.
- Budget cap, actual token count, and actual settled amount.
- Network, asset, tx hash, and settle status.
- Provider identity and advertised name.
- Payer and provider wallet/key identities.
- Timing fields such as `openedAt`, `completedAt`, and `settledAt`.
- Retry metadata when settlement is still being retried.

These are the rows that back the `/economy` ledger and `/economy/receipts` page.

## Trust inputs

Public routing uses more than price. A cheap provider can still lose if it is slow, unreliable, or has poor history.

| Signal | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Identity | Prevents anonymous capacity from blending into trusted private peers. |
| Reputation | Lets successful paid sessions and delivery history improve future ranking. |
| Receipts | Proves what was charged and whether settlement closed cleanly or is retrying. |
| Settlement result | Confirms paid runs closed on the expected rail. |
| Self-dealing checks | Detects when the same operator controls both sides of a paid route. |

## Paid evidence

Paid runs should leave two layers of evidence:

- **Runtime evidence** in the Leash and Hypha logs: quote, authorization, stream, meter, close.
- **Settlement evidence** on the configured payment rail: the final transaction or facilitator result.

The provider earns only after the metered close succeeds.

## Free-route evidence

Free public routes and private routes still matter, but the current economy receipt surface is not
where they show up. Today, validate them through:

- Conductor route decisions and mesh event traces.
- Hypha peer and capability snapshots.
- Public-vs-private routing behavior in the chat flow.

Do not expect zero-rate runs to appear as the same replicated settlement receipt rows used for paid sessions.
