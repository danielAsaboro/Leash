---
title: "What is Leash?"
description: "A private, on-device AI assistant with access to your world — no cloud, ever."
---

Leash is a personal AI assistant that runs **entirely on your own hardware**. Every model
call — chat, reasoning, embeddings, retrieval, speech-to-text, text-to-speech, image
generation — happens locally through the [QVAC SDK](https://qvac.tether.io). Nothing you
say, store, or ask ever reaches a third-party API.

It is the flagship surface of **Mycelium**, an exocortex that spans your personal device
mesh: pair a second machine and they share an encrypted, peer-to-peer brain — one device
can borrow another's GPU for heavy turns without any traffic leaving your mesh.

## The rules it lives by

- **Local-first, always.** All inference goes through a local QVAC serve. There is no
  cloud fallback to misconfigure.
- **Offline-capable.** After a one-time model download, everything except web research
  works in airplane mode.
- **Honest by construction.** Failures surface as errors in the UI — never silent
  catches, never hallucinated success.
- **Human-in-the-loop for side effects.** Anything that writes to your machine, your
  smart home, or executes code pauses on an Approve/Deny card first.
- **Open source.** Apache-2.0, reproducible, with audit logs for every model call.
