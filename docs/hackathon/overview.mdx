## Leash in one screen

Leash is a private, offline-capable assistant that runs entirely on your own hardware through `@qvac/sdk` — no cloud AI in the loop. It grounds answers in your notes, screen activity, and past conversations; drives your machine with your approval; borrows compute and modalities from your other devices over an encrypted mesh; settles that borrowed compute on-chain when you turn the economy on; and improves itself overnight with an on-device LoRA loop. Underneath sits **Mycelium** — the five-layer engine (mesh, senses, mind, memory, clients) that hosts all of it.

It is not a single demo trick. It is a composing system: chat, RAG, vision, speech, computer use, a device mesh, an agent economy, a skills harness, and a self-improvement loop, all on the same QVAC-only runtime. See [What Leash is](/explanation/what-mycelium-is) for the product/engine split and [Architecture](/explanation/architecture) for the layers.

<Note>
**The QVAC-only rule.** Every inference, embedding, RAG retrieval, and multimodal call routes through `@qvac/sdk` to a local serve. There are no OpenAI/Anthropic/cloud-AI calls anywhere in the system — a single one would disqualify the submission. [See the proof →](/hackathon/qvac-only-proof)
</Note>

## Track fit: General Purpose

The hackathon defines four device-class tracks. Leash targets **General Purpose** — retail devices with ≤32 GB RAM (laptops, desktops, workstations, mini-PCs):

- **Primary device:** an Apple-Silicon Mac mini (≤32 GB), running the full stack.
- **Peers:** additional Macs used as delegation and economy peers over the mesh.

The General Purpose track explicitly calls for multi-agent orchestration, multimodal work, advanced RAG, P2P delegation, fine-tuning/LoRA, and privacy-first tooling — which is the exact surface area Leash exercises.

## A built-in Psy-Models angle

The hackathon also runs a **Psy Models** track for meaningful use of QVAC's MedPsy/MedGemma. Leash serves `medpsy` — QVAC's **MedGemma 4B IT** — as a first-class alias and routes to it automatically on health intent, with a dashboard-editable prompt suffix. So the same General Purpose build also demonstrates the Psy-Models capability without a separate project; see [Models and the catalog](/explanation/models-and-the-catalog).

## Offline acceptance

After a one-time warm-cache (first run downloads the GGUF weights and bootstraps the mesh, online), everything runs with no network. The acceptance test is literal: **it works in airplane mode**. There's no login, no API key, no cloud dependency on the hot path.
